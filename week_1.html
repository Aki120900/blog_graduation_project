Week 1 – Ideation & Planning

Published: 2025-02-21 | Tags: planning, project, ideation

This week I began shaping the initial concept for my graduation project. Inspired by the city of Omashu from Avatar: The Last Airbender, I explored how its earthbending systems could be reinterpreted as adaptive architecture in real life.

The goal is to develop a 3D animated simulation of a modular, disaster-responsive city using Maya. The idea will blend fantasy aesthetics with real-world data and architectural theory to explore resilience in urban environments.

I’m feeling confident that this idea will allow me to combine both technical and conceptual skills.

🏁 Project Purpose

To reimagine how adaptive architecture can mitigate the effects of natural disasters like earthquakes and tsunamis—drawing inspiration from the modular, kinetic infrastructure of Omashu (Avatar: The Last Airbender), and blending it with real-world seismic data, architectural theory, and computational design in Autodesk Maya.

🔍 Possible Real-World Anchors:

  • Modular housing (e.g., disaster relief shelters)
  • Self-shifting infrastructure (inspired by tectonic plate movement)
  • Nature-mimicking design (biomimicry: termite mounds, coral reefs, tree root stability)

Can architecture inspired by fantasy and grounded in science help cities survive natural disasters?

🧩 Main Components

🎬 Maya-Based Simulation

  • 3D animated city inspired by Omashu
  • Dynamic building reactions to earthquakes, floods, tsunamis
  • Rigging and animation tied to real seismic data curves
  • Biomimicry-based kinetic systems

🌍 Data-Driven Integration

  • Use real seismic/environmental data (USGS, NASA)
  • Animate behaviors based on this data
  • Visualize results through graphs, overlays, and effects

📚 Architectural & Scientific Theory

  • Biomimicry
  • Seismic structural systems
  • Kinetic/adaptive architecture

Cited research integrated into thesis and design reasoning

🌐 Final Presentation: Website

  • Maya renders & animations
  • Data visualizations
  • Research synthesis
  • Interactive sections
  • Downloadable thesis

📽️ How Will You Present It?

  • Interactive desktop simulation or screen-recorded video
  • Voiceover/narration for scientific explanation
  • Optional short interactive webpage/app to complement video

🎯 Target Audience & Impact

  • Urban designers & architects
  • Disaster response planners
  • Students & educators in environmental design
  • Creative technologists exploring speculative urbanism

Impact: This can spark new ways of thinking about future cities—especially in vulnerable regions. It's also a proof of concept that creative computing can bridge imagination + engineering.

🛠️ Tools

  • Maya – modeling, rigging, animation
  • Bifrost / MASH – simulations
  • Python / Excel – data prep
  • Figma / Canva – visuals
  • VSCode / HTML/CSS/JS – website
  • Netlify / GitHub Pages / Vercel – hosting
  • Zotero / Mendeley – citation management

📌 Suggested Workflow

Phase 1: Research & Planning (Week 1–4)

  • Define focus & feasibility
  • Create timeline & thesis outline
  • Deliverables: SMART goals, MoSCoW, Gantt, sketches, personas

Phase 2: Data & Simulation (Week 4–6)

  • Collect & visualize real data
  • Animate building responses
  • Deliverables: charts, CSVs, prototype scenes

Phase 3: City Design & Rigging (Week 6–9)

  • Model city & modular components
  • Rig & animate disaster systems
  • Deliverables: animation sequences, defense systems

Phase 4: Rendering & Web Build (Week 9–11)

  • Render final video, build site
  • Deliverables: video, visual assets, live website

Phase 5: Final Documentation (Week 11–13)

  • Write 5,000-word thesis
  • Document entire process
  • Deliverables: thesis PDF, appendices, live demo link
