Week 8 – Visual Research, Concept Design & 3D Modelling

Tags: Blender, concept design, low-poly, environment, 3D modelling

This week I shifted from data-driven simulations to a more visually oriented design process. Rather than using real datasets, I focused on building a believable environment through strong reference research, stylised modelling, and environmental storytelling.

I began by exploring various platforms for inspiration—500px, ArtStation, DeviantArt, Pixiv, and Pinterest—searching for landscape compositions, architectural styles, and colour palettes. Once I had gathered enough references, I used DALL·E to generate specific concept sketches based on my vision for the city. These AI-generated visuals helped solidify ideas for my 3D design and gave me a creative baseline for building assets.

From there, I began modelling in Blender, starting with a low-poly house design. Once the core structure was complete, I explored different websites to gather high-quality materials and assets to enhance the model. I used Poly Haven to source textures and furniture elements, which added realism and depth to the house interior and exterior.

With the building in place, I turned my attention to the landscape design. I conducted additional research and a brief literature review on unusual urban topographies. Inspired by the Azores Islands, which are built on volcanic land yet still support thriving communities, I decided to locate my fictional city on a mountainous terrain. This choice supports the project’s narrative goal—to communicate that not all cities are built in ideal or safe locations, yet urban life adapts and flourishes regardless.

This week laid a strong foundation for both the architectural style and environmental identity of my fictional city.
