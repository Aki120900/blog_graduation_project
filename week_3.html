Week 3 – SMART Goals, MoSCoW & Gantt Chart

Published: 2025-03-07 | Tags: planning, management

This week was all about project management.

I created SMART goals to define key milestones and ensure that each task is achievable within my timeline. I also used the MoSCoW method to prioritise project features and deliverables—from essential animations and data visualisations to optional extras like voiceovers and interactive elements.

A Gantt chart has been built to visualise the full development process, and it’s already helping me manage scope and workflow expectations.

These goals are now aligned with the revised development plan and ensure I stay on track.

📌 MoSCoW Prioritisation

To stay focused and ensure I'm developing the most critical elements first, I’ve used the MoSCoW method to prioritise features and deliverables for my graduation project: Seismic City – A Data-Driven Maya Simulation of Adaptive Architecture.

Priority Feature / Deliverable
Must Have Maya-based 3D animation simulating disaster response, seismic data input driving movement, final project website, and 5,000-word thesis
Should Have User persona profiles, data overlays and graphs, infographic breakdowns of animation logic
Could Have Voiceover narration or audio design, animated interface mockups, environmental soundscapes
Won’t Have Real-time 3D city viewer, AR feature, Unity/VR prototype (outside current scope)

By focusing first on the “Must Haves”, I ensure the core simulation experience—based on architectural theory and seismic data—is successfully realised in Maya and clearly communicated through a fully documented website and thesis.
